# QuarryChain Brand Voice Guidelines
Version 1.0 — April 2026

## 1. Brand Overview

QuarryChain is a Layer 1 blockchain built on Delegated Proof of Stake (DPoS) consensus. The network delivers 100,000 TPS, 3-second finality, and full EVM compatibility. Its ecosystem spans QuarrySwap (DEX), QuarryWallet, QuarryScan (explorer), QVM (virtual machine), QRC-20 token standards, and real-world asset tokenization.

The brand voice is the voice of engineers who built something that works — and know it. No evangelical pitch, no breathless hype, no nervous justification. QuarryChain states facts, shows capability, and moves on.

**Positioning:** The production-ready Layer 1 for builders who are done compromising on throughput, finality, or tooling familiarity.

**Tone anchor:** Solana homepage energy — not IBM, not Cardano, not a DeFi influencer's Twitter feed.

## 2. We Are / We Are Not

| Dimension | We Are | We Are Not |
|---|---|---|
| Confidence | We state facts plainly. "100,000 TPS. 3-second finality." | Not arrogant. We do not mock competitors. Never "unlike Ethereum." |
| Technical depth | We speak precisely. DPoS, not "community voting." QVM, not "our Ethereum-like system." | Not impenetrable. Jargon serves clarity, not gatekeeping. |
| Energy | Focused. Purposeful. A builder shipping, not a marketer pitching. | Not hype-bro. Not exclamation marks. Not "This is HUGE." Not "GM." |
| Tone register | Punchy. Minimal. Copy that costs a day to make look effortless. | Not corporate. Not committee-written. No qualifiers: "potentially," "could be," "we believe." |
| Audience | We respect the reader's intelligence. They know what 100K TPS means. | We do not over-explain. We do not walk them through why blockchain matters. |
| Stance on comparison | We show what we do. Full stop. | We do not explain why competitors are slow. Solana doesn't explain why Ethereum is slow. |
| Claims | Every claim is backed by a number, a mechanism, or a product that exists. | We do not hallucinate social proof. |
| CTAs | Action verbs. Statements. "Start Building." "Read the Docs." | Never questions. Never "Ready to build?" Never "Want to learn more?" |
| Design voice | Dark. Precise. Subtle glow. Wireframe geometry. No decorative fuss. | No clipart. No light mode. No drop shadows. No rainbow gradients on body text. |

## 3. Voice Constants vs. Tone Flexes

### Voice Constants (Never Change)
- **State, do not ask.** Every piece of copy ends with momentum, not a question.
- **Stats are sacred.** 100,000 TPS. 3-second finality. 27 elected validators. 0.25% fee. Always exact.
- **No qualifier words.** Banned: "potentially," "could," "might," "we believe," "really," "very," "innovative," "revolutionary," "game-changing," "next-generation."
- **Product names are proper.** QuarryChain, QuarrySwap, QuarryWallet, QuarryScan, QVM, QRC-20, QRY.
- **Two sentences maximum per feature card.**
- **No comparison framing.** Show what you are. Never frame against what others are not.

### Tone Flexes (Adapt by Context)

| Dimension | Minimum | Maximum |
|---|---|---|
| Formality | Conversational but precise (social) | Measured and exact (whitepaper) |
| Technical depth | Stat-level only (homepage hero) | Full mechanism explanation (whitepaper) |
| Sentence length | 3–4 words (labels, CTAs) | 30–40 words (whitepaper body) |
| Warmth | Lean and direct (developer docs) | Slightly warmer but never soft (investor pitch) |
| Urgency | Low — confidence does not need urgency | Moderate — cold outreach may note timing |

## 4. Tone-by-Context Matrix

| Context | Formality | Energy | Technical Depth | CTA Style | Avoid |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Website (homepage) | Low-medium | Controlled confidence | Stat-level | Imperative statements | Walls of text, educational framing, problem/solution |
| Website (subpages) | Medium | Focused | Mechanism-level | Action verbs | Over-explaining what the homepage already previewed |
| Pitch deck | Medium | Quiet authority | Accessible to non-engineers | Specific asks | Superlatives without backup, "disrupting" anything |
| Whitepaper | High | Neutral | Maximum | N/A | Marketing language, padding, passive voice stacking |
| Blog | Medium-low | Curious and direct | Variable by post | Inline links | Listicle filler, "In the fast-moving world of..." |
| Social (X/LinkedIn) | Low | Punchy, hook first line | Surface unless technical thread | Short, specific | GM/GN, excessive emoji, vague hype |
| Cold outreach | Medium | Direct, first sentence = reason | Minimal unless technical recipient | 20-min ask | "I hope this finds you well," fake urgency |

## 6. Content Patterns

### Headlines
- Declarative: "The Blockchain Built for What's Next"
- Capability: "Your Solidity contracts work natively."
- Terse: "One chain. Everything you need."
- Principle: "Decentralized by design."
- Imperative: "Start building."
- Friction-removal: "Three steps. That's it."
- **Never:** rhetorical questions, vague aspiration, comparison framing

### Feature Descriptions
Two sentences max. Mechanism first, stat/differentiator second.
- "Community-elected validators. 6-hour voting rounds. Energy-efficient block production."
- "Non-custodial AMM with liquidity pools, yield farming, and AI-powered contract auditing."

### CTAs
Imperative verbs. Specific over generic. No questions. No "Today!" No exclamation marks.
- Approved: "Read the Docs" / "Start Building" / "Join Discord" / "Explore Testnet"
- Rejected: "Learn More" / "Get Started" / "Ready to build?"

### Labels
Uppercase, JetBrains Mono, tracking-widest. Teal for primary, blue for ecosystem.
- "GOVERNANCE" / "BUILD ON QUARRYCHAIN" / "QUICK START" / "ECOSYSTEM"

### Gradient Text
Teal→blue gradient on the most semantically important word/phrase in a headline. One per section. Not decorative.

## 7. Visual Voice

- **Dark mode = philosophy.** Precision, control, technical seriousness. Background #08080f (blue undertone, not flat black).
- **Space Grotesk = brand voice.** Geometric, technical. Headlines only.
- **Inter = content.** Disappears correctly. Body text.
- **JetBrains Mono = authority.** Stats in mono read as measurements, not marketing. Labels carry the weight of a gauge reading.
- **Color is semantic.** Teal = primary/governance. Blue = actions/links. Green = live/success. Red = alerts/emphasis. Never arbitrary.
- **Glow, not shadow.** Subtle radial glows = confident presence without heaviness.
- **Lucide line icons only.** No clipart. Every icon defensible by semantic accuracy.
- **Wireframe geometry = inspectable architecture.** Solid shape = black box. Wireframe = a system you can audit.
- **Animation earns its place.** NumberTicker = live data. HexGrid wave = network activity. Node ring rotation = governance always moving. No decorative motion.

## 8. Open Questions

1. **Community warmth ceiling** — How far does warmth flex in Discord/AMAs? Is there a distinct community persona?
2. **Comparison rule in pitch decks** — When an investor names Solana directly, is a factual comparison acceptable in conversation?
3. **Press placement threshold** — Must "As Seen In" have live article URLs, or are confirmed forthcoming mentions acceptable?
4. **Token marketing voice** — ICO/presale communications: same system with tighter scrutiny, or separate voice?
5. **Blog sub-types** — Technical deep-dives, ecosystem updates, and educational posts: three sub-contexts or one flexible blog voice?
6. **AI claims in marketing** — QuarrySwap's AI audit feature: does every mention need an explanatory note?
7. **"Quarry Miners" vs "validators"** — Consumer-facing governance copy: always Quarry Miners, or validators acceptable in summaries?
